=== PREVENTIVE AND PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE TECHNICAL STRATEGY ===

This document details the preventive and predictive maintenance strategies, continuous sensor monitoring protocols, and critical mechanical wear indicators used to optimize factory floor reliability.

1. CRITICAL SENSOR MONITORING PROTOCOLS
Continuous telemetry tracking enables real-time health assessment and prevents catastrophic equipment breakdowns:
- Temperature Sensors: Dual RTD (Resistance Temperature Detector) sensors are embedded in the motor stator windings and bearing housings. Thermal monitoring catches localized cooling failures, overloaded winding states, or dry bearing friction early.
- Vibration Accelerometers: High-frequency triaxial accelerometers are mounted on the drive-end and non-drive-end bearing housings. Vibration velocity (measured in mm/s RMS) and high-frequency acceleration envelope spectra are captured continuously.
- Acoustic Emissions: Ultrasonic acoustic sensors are placed near low-speed rotating bearings to detect micro-cracks, mechanical stress waves, and early-stage structural spalling that standard vibration analysis might miss.
- Electrical Signature Analysis (ESA): Current clamps monitor the three-phase AC input current waveform to identify current imbalances, rotor bar damage, and stator winding insulation leakage.

2. PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE STRATEGIES
Rather than performing maintenance on a rigid calendar schedule (preventive), predictive maintenance schedules repairs based on the actual measured condition of the asset:
- Trend Analysis: Telemetry data must be trended over time. A slow, steady rise in bearing vibration velocity from 1.5 mm/s to 4.5 mm/s over several months indicates gradual wear, allowing technicians to schedule a replacement during a planned shutdown.
- Threshold-Based Alarms: The control system triggers automatic alerts when specific boundaries are crossed:
  * Vibration Velocity Alert: 4.5 mm/s RMS (Schedule inspection within 7 days).
  * Vibration Velocity Danger Limit: 7.1 mm/s RMS (Immediate controlled machine shutdown).
  * Winding Temperature Alert: 125 degrees Celsius.
  * Winding Temperature Critical Trip: 140 degrees Celsius.

3. CRITICAL WEAR INDICATORS AND INSPECTION CRITERIA
During planned inspections, technicians must check for these specific indicators of wear:
- Shaft Coupling Wear: Inspect the elastomeric inserts (spider couplings) for visible cracks, rubber shredding, or dry rotting. Measure coupling backlash; backlash exceeding 0.5 degrees requires coupling spider replacement.
- Gearbox Backlash and Oil Condition: Check gear backlash using a dial indicator. Analyze gearbox oil for metal particulates (iron, brass) which indicate severe gear face wear or bearing cage degradation.
- Structural Belts and Sheaves: Check V-belts for cracking, side-wall polishing, and proper tension. Use a laser alignment tool to check sheave alignments; misalignment exceeding 0.1 degrees must be corrected.
- Tool and Cutter Wear: For cutting systems, measure the edge radius of the blades. Blade edges showing more than 0.2mm of rounding or micro-chipping must be reground or replaced immediately to prevent motor overload.
