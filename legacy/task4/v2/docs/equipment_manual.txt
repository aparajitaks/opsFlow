=== HIGH-PERFORMANCE ELECTRIC MOTOR USER MANUAL ===

Model Series: HPM-400-3P
Category: Heavy-Duty Industrial Three-Phase Induction Motor

This manual provides the technical specifications, operating limits, and performance thresholds for the Model HPM-400-3P induction motor. All engineering personnel must memorize these operating limits to ensure safe and efficient operation.

1. STANDARD ELECTRIC MOTOR SPECIFICATIONS
- Rated Power: 300 kW (400 HP)
- Nominal Voltage: 460 V AC, 3-Phase, 60 Hz
- Full Load Current: 480 A
- Frame Size: 449T (Totally Enclosed Fan Cooled - TEFC)
- Insulation Class: Class H (Rated up to 180 degrees Celsius)
- Ingress Protection: IP55 (Dust-protected and water-jet resistant)

2. OPERATING TEMPERATURE RANGES AND LIMITS
Thermal management is critical to protecting winding insulation:
- Normal Operating Range: The motor is designed to operate continuously at internal winding temperatures between 60 degrees Celsius and 115 degrees Celsius, assuming a standard ambient air temperature of 25 degrees Celsius.
- Warning Threshold: If the winding temperature sensor registers 125 degrees Celsius, the control system will trigger a high-temperature warning alarm on the telemetry screen.
- Overheating Shutoff Limit: The maximum absolute temperature limit for Class H winding insulation is 140 degrees Celsius. Operating the motor above this temperature will result in rapid winding insulation degradation. If the sensor registers 140 degrees Celsius or higher, the automated safety circuit must instantly trip the motor's power supply to prevent permanent damage or electrical fire.

3. ROTATIONAL SPEED LIMITS
Rotational speed limits prevent mechanical imbalances and bearing disintegration:
- Nominal Speed: 1750 rpm (Rotational Speed) under standard 60 Hz full-load conditions.
- Minimum Continuous Operating Speed: 400 rpm. Operating below this speed without auxiliary forced ventilation cooling is prohibited, as the shaft-mounted fan will fail to generate sufficient airflow to cool the windings under load.
- Maximum Safe Rotational Speed: 2200 rpm. Operating the motor above this limit creates severe radial centrifugal stress and imbalance forces, leading to high vibration, bearing failure, or shaft deformation.

4. TORQUE THRESHOLDS AND OVERLOAD REGIMES
Managing torque load prevents structural twisting and winding burnout:
- Nominal Full-Load Torque: 1630 Nm (Newton-meters) at rated speed.
- Maximum Continuous Torque Threshold: 1800 Nm. This is the absolute upper limit for long-term operations.
- Intermittent Peak Torque Limit: 2200 Nm. The motor can sustain peak loads up to this torque threshold for no more than 60 seconds. Prolonged operation in this overload regime causes rapid heat dissipation breakdown and winding thermal collapse.
- Locked-Rotor Torque: 3200 Nm. Under zero-speed stall conditions, torque will spike immediately, drawing excessive locked-rotor current. The protective overcurrent relay must trip the circuit within 3 seconds of a locked-rotor event.
